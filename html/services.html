What are the effects of climate change?

Global climate change is not a future problem. Addressing climate change requires global cooperation, policy changes, technological advancements, and individual actions. International agreements like the Paris Agreement aim to limit global temperature rise and facilitate climate action. Transitioning to a low-carbon and sustainable future is crucial for mitigating the effects of climate change and ensuring a habitable planet for future generations.

Greenhouse Effect

Certain gases, known as greenhouse gases, trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, acting like a blanket. This natural process, called the greenhouse effect, is essential for life on Earth. However, human activities have significantly increased greenhouse gas emissions, leading to an enhanced greenhouse effect and global warming.
Global Warming
The increase in greenhouse gases has caused the Earth's average surface temperature to rise, leading to global warming. This rise in temperature has far-reaching effects on the environment, weather patterns, and ecosystems.
Melting Ice
As global temperatures increase, glaciers and ice sheets are melting at an accelerated rate. This contributes to rising sea levels, which pose a significant threat to coastal communities and low-lying areas. Increased coastal flooding and erosion are among the consequences.
Ocean Acidification

The excess carbon dioxide emitted into the atmosphere is absorbed by the oceans, leading to ocean acidification. This process makes seawater more acidic, which negatively impacts marine ecosystems, including coral reefs, shellfish, and other marine organisms.

Biodiversity Loss
Climate change disrupts ecosystems and threatens biodiversity. Many species face challenges such as habitat loss, reduced food availability, and altered migration patterns, which can lead to population decline and extinction.
Health Impacts
Climate change has implications for human health. Increased heatwaves can cause heat-related illnesses and deaths, while altered rainfall patterns can affect water and food supplies, leading to malnutrition and the spread of vector-borne diseases like malaria and dengue fever.
