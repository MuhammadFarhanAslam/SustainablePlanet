Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns that can be natural, due to changes in the sun’s activity or large volcanic eruptions.

Climate Change

Human activities, principally through emissions of greenhouse gases, have unequivocally caused global warming, with global surface temperature reaching 1.1°C above 1850–1900 in 2011–2020.

Climate Change

Global greenhouse gas emissions have continued to increase, with unequal historical and ongoing contributions arising from unsustainable energy use, land use and land-use change, lifestyles and patterns of consumption and production across regions, between and within countries, and among individuals

After Effects

Global climate change is not a future problem. Addressing climate change requires global cooperation, policy changes, technological advancements, and individual actions. International agreements like the Paris Agreement aim to limit global temperature rise and facilitate climate action. Transitioning to a low-carbon and sustainable future is crucial for mitigating the effects of climate change and ensuring a habitable planet for future generations.

Factors

Life on Earth depends on energy coming from the Sun. About half the light energy reaching Earth's atmosphere passes through the air and clouds to the surface, where it is absorbed and radiated in the form of infrared heat. About 90% of this heat is then absorbed by greenhouse gases and re-radiated, slowing heat loss to space.

Yes, The time is running Out!!

The world has already heated up by around 1.2C, on average, since the preindustrial era, pushing humanity beyond almost all historical boundaries. Cranking up the temperature of the entire globe this much within little more than a century is, in fact, extraordinary, with the oceans alone absorbing the heat equivalent of five Hiroshima atomic bombs dropping into the water every second.
